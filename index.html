Jonny Huber is a student at De La Salle High School. He is a sophomore and plays lacrosse and hockey for the school. He also has had an internship at the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory and at Uptempo Group-a software development company. Jonny currently works for Uptempo Tech where he teaches kids tech skills like working with CAD software, 3D Printing, and Coding.

Gallery

Here are the things that have been designed by Jonny so far this year. If you would like to see a larger version of any of the images click the image.

PHOTOSHOP EDIT 1

In this edit in Photoshop, the hood ornament of the car was edited to remove the worse looking parts of the arrow and the man. I used the spot healing brush tool and the patch tool.

PHOTOSHOP EDIT 2

In this edit in Photoshop, the multiple pictures of Monterey Bay Aquarium are combined in a collage and a rectangle with the words "Monterey, California" cut out.

PHOTOSHOP EDIT 3

In this edit in Photoshop Jonny took two different images and combined them to make it look like the car was orignially on that lawn. He did this by using a layer mask.

TYPOGRAPHY 1

This design of the word "create" was done in Adobe Illustrator. Jonny made a ruler in Illustrator and then made each leter from a few different rulers.

TYPOGRAPHY 2

This design of the word "create" was also done in Adobe Illustrator. Jonny made the word "create" to look like it was being 3D Printed.

CEREAL DRIVE POSTER

This poster was designed in Adobe Illustrator for the yearly cereal drive for people in need. Jonny designed the bowl, car, and spoon from scratch. He did this by taking shapes like circles, rectangles, and lines.

CUBISM

This poster was designed in Adobe Illustrator as a flyer for the games for a hockey team. Jonny made the background, hockey sticks, and puck by making the outline of the things, then dragging lines randomly around, and finally filling in the shapes with different colors.

LOGOS

These logos were made in Adobe Illustrator. They are Wordmark, Symbol, and Combination in color and black and white variants.
